FinFlow — Finance Dashboard

An end-to-end UX case study for a personal finance management app, focused on helping users understand their spending habits at a glance.

Year

2024

Role

UX Researcher & UI Designer

Tools

Figma, FigJam, Maze

Overview

FinFlow started as a personal interest project after I noticed how hard it was to get a clear picture of monthly spending using existing banking apps. Most of them show transactions as a long list, which makes it difficult to spot patterns without a lot of manual effort.

The goal of this project was to design a dashboard that puts the most important information front and center — what you spent, where, and how it compares to previous months — all without overwhelming the user.

Process

01

User Research

Conducted 5 semi-structured interviews with people who track their finances. The main pain point that came up consistently was that existing tools feel like they're built for accountants, not regular people.

02

Wireframing

Sketched and then digitised low-fidelity wireframes in FigJam, focusing on the information hierarchy. Went through three rounds of iteration before settling on a layout that balanced charts and text.

03

High-Fidelity Prototype

Built a clickable prototype in Figma covering the main dashboard, transaction view, and a monthly breakdown screen. Used a dark colour scheme to reduce eye strain for frequent use.

04

Usability Testing

Ran 4 usability tests with Maze. The biggest issue found was that the category breakdown chart wasn't clear enough — I redesigned the legend and added labels directly on the chart segments.

Outcome

The final prototype covers the core spending dashboard, a detailed category view, and a monthly comparison screen. The biggest design challenge was deciding how much data to show without making the screen feel cluttered.

After the usability revisions, all 4 test participants were able to complete the core tasks (finding monthly spending, checking top categories) without any guidance. Two of them said it was "the clearest finance interface they had used."
